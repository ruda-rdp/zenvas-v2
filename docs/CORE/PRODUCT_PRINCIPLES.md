# Purpose

Product Principles define **how product decisions are made** — not what Zenvas
is (that's Foundation) or what Zenvas believes (that's Philosophy).

Whenever a new feature is proposed, it should be evaluated against the five
principles below before being built.

This document intentionally does not repeat concepts already defined
elsewhere. The Decision Checklist references those concepts by name instead
of redefining them, in service of "One Concept, One Home" (see
ZENVAS_DOCUMENTATION_MAP.md).

---

# 1. Service First

Projects are the result of a Service. Services create relationships.
Relationships create sustainable businesses.

Every Brand starts by defining its Service Catalog. Orders, Projects, and
everything downstream flow from Service — not the other way around. Zenvas is
designed around delivering exceptional creative services, not simply managing
tasks.

---

# 2. Framework, Not Workflow

Every creative business works differently. Zenvas provides a flexible
operational framework rather than forcing a fixed workflow.

Brands are free to define their own packages, approval process, SLA,
production pipeline, internal SOP, and customer journey. Zenvas provides the
foundation; Brands define how they operate.

---

# 3. Human Before Software

Software should adapt to people. People should not be forced to adapt to
software.

If a feature adds friction, the feature is wrong — not the person struggling
to use it. Technology should amplify human creativity, not force behavior
change.

---

# 4. Creativity Without Chaos

Creative work is naturally dynamic. Operations should not be.

Every product decision should protect this boundary: preserve creative
freedom on one side, and operational stability and predictability on the
other. A feature that makes creative work more rigid, or operations more
unpredictable, has failed this principle regardless of its other merits.

---

# 5. Build for the Next Ten Years

Zenvas should avoid trends that quickly become obsolete.

Every major product decision should answer: "Will this still make sense five
to ten years from now?" If not, rethink the decision.

---

# Decision Checklist

Before building any feature, ask:

```
✓ Does it strengthen the Brand, not replace it?
    → BUSINESS_OS.md (Brand First)

✓ Does it remain invisible to clients?
    → BUSINESS_OS.md (Invisible Infrastructure)

✓ Is it part of the framework, not forcing one workflow?
    → Principle #2 (this document)

✓ Does it strengthen long-term relationships over single transactions?
    → BUSINESS_OS.md (Relationship Over Transactions)

✓ Does it reduce operational chaos as the business scales?
    → FOUNDATION.md (Growth Philosophy) + PHILOSOPHY.md

✓ Does it keep knowledge with the organization, not the individual?
    → FOUNDATION.md (Organization Memory)

✓ Does the project survive if the people involved change?
    → BUSINESS_OS.md (Operational Continuity)

✓ Does it help people think and work better, without replacing judgment?
    → PHILOSOPHY.md (People create great work; AI amplifies people)

✓ Will this still make sense 5-10 years from now?
    → Principle #5 (this document)
```

If most answers are "No," the feature should be reconsidered.

---

# Closing Statement

Zenvas is not built to become the center of attention. It is built to quietly
enable creative businesses to operate with confidence, consistency, and
clarity. When Brands grow stronger, teams become calmer, and clients return
without friction, Zenvas has fulfilled its purpose.
